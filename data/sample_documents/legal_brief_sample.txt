LEGAL BRIEF: DATA PRIVACY COMPLIANCE ASSESSMENT

PREPARED FOR: Board of Directors
DATE: February 2026
CLASSIFICATION: Confidential

BACKGROUND
This brief assesses the company's compliance posture with respect to Mexico's Federal Law on Protection of Personal Data (LFPDPPP), GDPR requirements for European clients, and emerging AI regulation frameworks.

CURRENT STATUS
The company processes personal data of approximately 45,000 individuals across Mexico, the United States, and the European Union. Current data handling practices meet basic LFPDPPP requirements but fall short of GDPR Article 30 record-keeping obligations.

KEY FINDINGS
1. Data Processing Records: Incomplete documentation of processing activities. Deadline for compliance is March 15, 2026.
2. Consent Management: Current consent mechanisms need updating to meet GDPR explicit consent standards.
3. AI Model Training: Use of customer data for AI model training requires additional privacy impact assessment.
4. Cross-Border Transfers: Data transfers between Mexico and EU lack adequate Standard Contractual Clauses.
5. Data Breach Response: Incident response plan exists but has not been tested in 18 months.

RISK ASSESSMENT
- HIGH RISK: GDPR non-compliance for EU clients (potential fine up to 4% of annual revenue)
- MEDIUM RISK: LFPDPPP record-keeping gaps (regulatory scrutiny increasing)
- LOW RISK: US data handling (no federal privacy law yet, state laws being monitored)

RECOMMENDATIONS
Immediate action is required to address HIGH risk items before the March 2026 deadline. Budget allocation of $150,000 is recommended for compliance remediation, external audit, and legal counsel.

This brief is privileged and confidential legal communication.
